# Aluminium Industry: Extraction and Sustainability Analysis

A detailed study of the aluminium production lifecycle, covering bauxite mining, alumina extraction, electrolytic reduction, and recycling processes, with a focus on environmental and energy considerations.

## Project Overview
This project analyzes the complete industrial pathway of aluminium production, starting from bauxite ore to final metal extraction. It also evaluates sustainability aspects such as waste generation, energy consumption, and recycling efficiency.

The study integrates materials science fundamentals with process-level understanding to highlight key challenges and improvements in the aluminium industry.

## Key Features
- End-to-end analysis of aluminium production from bauxite to metal
- Detailed study of Bayer Process (alumina extraction)
- Analysis of Hall-Héroult process (electrolytic reduction)
- Evaluation of aluminium recycling and secondary production
- Identification of environmental challenges (red mud, emissions, waste)
- Comparison of primary vs secondary aluminium production

## Methodology
- Studied compositional characteristics of bauxite (Al₂O₃, Fe₂O₃, SiO₂, TiO₂)
- Analyzed process flow: Bauxite → Bayer Process → Alumina → Electrolysis
- Examined electrochemical reactions in Hall-Héroult process
- Evaluated energy requirements and thermodynamic constraints
- Investigated recycling processes and material recovery techniques
- Assessed environmental impact and mitigation strategies

## Key Observations
- Aluminium extraction requires high energy due to strong Al–O bonding
- Hall-Héroult process operates at ~950°C using molten cryolite
- Recycling consumes ~5% of the energy compared to primary production
- Red mud (bauxite residue) poses major environmental challenges
- Waste management and recycling significantly improve sustainability

## Applications
- Aerospace and lightweight structural components
- Electrical transmission systems
- Transportation and automotive sectors
- Packaging and consumer products
